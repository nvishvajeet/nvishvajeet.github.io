Research

Qubit's research programme is organised along the four verticals of the National Quantum Mission. Each vertical pairs a simulation track (virtual exploration on cloud hardware) with a table-top experimental track, so that undergraduate students and early-career researchers can move between theory, computation, and measurement.

Quantum computing

  • Algorithms and complexity — circuit design, simulation, verification
  • Near-term hardware — variational methods, noise and decoherence modelling
  • Cloud-hardware exercises — Grover search, Deutsch–Jozsa, VQE

Quantum communication

  • Quantum key distribution protocols and their implementation
  • Entanglement-based links and polarisation qubits

Quantum sensing

  • Table-top optics — interferometry, single-photon detection
  • Hong–Ou–Mandel interference and coincidence measurement

Quantum materials

  • Foundational experimentation in support of the above tracks
  • Electronic control modules for synchronisation and measurement

The research programme is embedded in MIT-WPU's UG Minor in Quantum Technologies (Track A: Virtual Exploration; Track B: Quantum Effects & Applications), and extends beyond it into student projects, seminars, and external collaboration.
