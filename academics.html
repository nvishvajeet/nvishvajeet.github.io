Academics

Here is the list of courses I have taken during my time at IIT Madras and TIFR. In case you want to learn what's taught in these courses, my two words - for most of the theoretical CS courses below, Prof. Prahladh Harsha's lecture notes are a really nice source. They dwell more on specific areas within complexity theory and newer results, and are more suited for graduate students who already know a thing or two. If you are an undergraduate student, or someone who is starting out in the area, following Prof. Jayalal Sarma's resources would help you get started on exploring the landscape, even though most of the references point to Prof. Prahladh's notes.


- Calculus of One Variable - Algorithmic Algebra
- Calculus of Multiple Variables - Advanced Theory of Computation
- Probability, Statistics and Stochastic Processes - Advanced Complexity Theory
- Introduction to Programming in C - Advanced Algorithms
- Probability Foundations (Measure Theoretic) - Advanced Topics in Communications
- Process Optimization - PCPs and Limits of Approximation Algorithms[1]
- Multivariate Data Analysis - Communication Complexity
- Foundations of Cryptography [2] - Distributed Network Algorithms : Foundations and Future Directions[2]

1. Offered by Prof. Prahladh Harsha at TIFR, Mumbai in May-June 2015. ↩

2. Ongoing course. ↩
